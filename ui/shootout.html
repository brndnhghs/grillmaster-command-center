🎬 Shootout — evolutionary nodegraph generator

How it works

1 · Generate. The system samples a batch of wild-but-valid node graphs (every connection is port-type-legal), renders each to a short looping clip, and culls the dead ones — black, frozen, pure flicker, or too slow to render. The survivors land in the grid.

2 · Rate & annotate. Give each clip 1–5 stars and, optionally, write pros/cons in its notes box — what works, what to change. Unrated clips are simply skipped.

3 · Evolve. Stars pick the parents (4–5★ dominate; the best clip carries over unchanged). Offspring are made by mutating one parent or splicing two together, plus a share of fresh random "explorers" so it never goes stale. Your notes are interpreted by the LLM advisor and steer the breeding: favor/avoid certain nodes, grow or simplify graphs, protect or drop specific clips — watch the 🧭 line for what it understood. Each clip now shows what was changed to it (mutation / crossover / explorer / protected) so you can see exactly how the breeding moved, and ~45% of every generation is fresh random exploration for a wide spread of results.

4 · Taste model. Every rating is logged to a permanent dataset. Train taste fits a model that predicts your stars (shown as ~n★ on new clips); it compounds across sessions.

5 · Keepers. Open in editor loads a genome into the node editor; Save puts it in the graph library. Both survive outside the loop.

Clips render server-side; a generation takes a few minutes depending on pool size, clip length, and render budget.
